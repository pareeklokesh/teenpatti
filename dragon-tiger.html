Dragon Tiger

Dragon Tiger is extremely simple to play. Two cards are dealt: one to the Dragon betting position on the table, and one to the Tiger betting position. The betting position that gets the higher card wins.

Cards & Table

When it comes to the cards, the game is played with 6 decks of cards, with no joker cards.

The values of the cards in Dragon Tiger are: Ace, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, B, J, Q and K. Ace is the lowest card with a value of 1. King is the highest card.

Rules

In each round, the dealer draws 2 cards. Your goal is to bet on which card will get the highest point: Dragon or Tiger. If you think Dragon wins, you place your bet on the Dragon square. If you think Tiger wins, you place your bet on the Tiger square to the right.

There is also an option to bet on a tie and suited tie.

As the player, you place your bets on the Dragon, the Tiger or the Tie.

Aces are the lowest ranking cards, with the rest of the cards following their natural values – two the next lowest, with king the highest card.

The highest card wins in Live Dragon Tiger, regardless of the suit.

In the case of a tie – both the Dragon position and Tiger position being dealt a card of the same value – players lose 50% of their bet on either Dragon or Tiger.

Winnings are paid for the winning bets at the end of each game round.
